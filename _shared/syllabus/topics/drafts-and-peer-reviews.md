# Drafts and Peer Reviews

Drafts and peer reviews are an important part of this class. For major assignments, you will submit a draft and peer review one or more classmates' drafts.

## Why do we do drafts and peer reviews?

- The purpose of the drafts is to encourage you to front-load the work as much as possible. Front-loading the draft gives you time to step away from the work for a day or two, and come back to revise with fresh eyes, and with peer feedback.
- The peer review has two purposes:
  - It gives you the opportunity to get feedback from others on your work.
  - It gives you the opportunity to see how others are approaching the writing task. This change of perspective can be just as important as the feedback you receive from others.

## Your role in the drafts and peer reviews

Your role is to turn in a draft and complete assigned peer reviews. When you complete your peer reviews, please be sure to do the following two things:

- [Complete the rubric in Canvas](https://community.canvaslms.com/t5/Student-Guide/How-do-I-submit-a-peer-review-to-an-assignment/ta-p/293)
- Leave a helpful end comment. (See Below)

## Late Drafts

Shortly after the due date for the draft, Canvas will assign peer review partners automatically. If you turn your draft in late, you will not be assigned a peer review partner. If this happens, go ahead and submit your draft and then [email me](mailto:josh.welsh@cwu.edu), so that I can go in to Canvas and set you up with peer review partners. I can't guarantee that you will receive feedback on a late draft, but you will be able to provide feedback to others.

## Writing Helpful Feedback for your Peer Review Partners

One of the best ways to give feedback is to follow the "Describe, Evaluate, Suggest" model:

- **Describe** – say what you see as a reader
- **Evaluate** – explain how the text meets or doesn't meet criteria established in the prompt
- **Suggest** – offer concrete advice for improvement
