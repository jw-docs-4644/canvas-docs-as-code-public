# Use of ChatGPT or other Generative AI Tools

Artificial Intelligence tools have their place in the professional writing world, and we are still learning what that place will be. For this class, it may be tempting to use AI to complete the small, regular writing assignments. I really hope you will choose not to do this, since those are "writing to learn" activities. In other words, it is through that writing that you’ll do much of the learning I hope you’ll do in this class. If you use AI to complete writing work in the class, then your learning will be hampered. 

For larger assignments, it may be helpful to use AI tools to generate competing perspectives or even to help with outlining. But any text that you produce and turn in for credit should be written entirely by you. Papers generated by AI tools will be considered plagiarized and will result in the consequences outlined in the Scholastic Integrity section of this syllabus.

We may do in-class or online exercises to help develop ethical ways of using AI tools. If you are uncomfortable using these tools, just let me know and we'll figure out alternative arrangement. 

You are not required to use AI to assist your writing. In many of the reflective essays you write, I will ask you to discuss your use of AI, if it was used. This is for me to get a sense of how people are using these tools and what benefits they see in them. I hope you will be honest and frank in your answers.
