# University Policies

**Academic Dishonesty:**

Students are on their honor to follow the student conduct code as outlined in the [Washington Administrative Code](http://apps.leg.wa.gov/WAC/default.aspx?cite=106-125). Violations of this section will result in a failing grade in the course in addition to further possible university sanctions.

Academic dishonesty is defined in the [Washington Administrative Code (WAC) 106-125-20](https://app.leg.wa.gov/WAC/default.aspx?cite=106-125)
Prohibited Student Conduct.

- Violation of an instructor's written policy on the use of artificial intelligence (AI) tools for coursework shall be considered an instance of academic dishonesty.
- Faculty who suspect students of academic dishonesty must follow the procedure outlined in CWUR 2-90-040(25).
- Students found responsible of academic dishonesty violations in a course will be prohibited from completing an SEOI for the course.
- Withdrawing from a course does not excuse academic dishonesty. In circumstances when academic dishonesty is confirmed, a W can be replaced by a letter grade (see CWUP 5-90-040(13)).

**Policy on Diversity:**

University-level education is about broadening horizons and looking at academic issues from a variety of perspectives. The participants in this class are encouraged to bring their own life experiences and viewpoints to bear on classroom discussions and assignments. Along with the freedom to express one's own views comes the responsibility to respect the views of others. No student will be discriminated against based on race, ethnicity, age, creed, religion, gender, sexual orientation, marital status, or political ideology.

**Sexual Misconduct & Student Support:**

- Central Washington University is committed to providing all community members with a learning and work environment that is free from [sexual misconduct](https://app.leg.wa.gov/WAC/default.aspx?cite=106-125-205&pdf=true) and discrimination.
- Students have [rights and options](https://online.flippingbook.com/view/855818969/) for getting help if they have experienced sexual misconduct or discrimination. Information can be found at the [Office for Civil Rights Compliance website](https://www.cwu.edu/about/offices/civil-rights-compliance/) or by contacting the [Title IX Coordinator](mailto:CRC@cwu.edu).
- As [Responsible Employees](https://www.cwu.edu/about/offices/civil-rights-compliance/addressing-preventing-sexual-misconduct/responsible-employee-reporting.php), all faculty and staff are required to report information regarding sexual misconduct or related incidents and disclosures regarding sexual misconduct and discrimination to the [Title IX Coordinator](mailto:CRC@cwu.edu), unless they are designated as a confidential resource.
- Students may receive confidential support by contacting [CWU PATH](https://www.cwu.edu/student-life/health-wellness-services/wellness/violence-response/index.php), 509-963-3213 or [PATH@cwu.edu](mailto:PATH@cwu.edu), or [CWU Student Counseling Services](https://www.cwu.edu/student-life/health-wellness-services/counseling-services/), 509-963-1391.

**Disability Services:**

Central Washington University is committed to creating a learning environment that meets the needs of its diverse student body. If you anticipate or experience any obstacles to learning, contact Disability Services to discuss a range of available options. Student Disability Services is located in Bouillon Hall 205. Call (509) 963-2214 or [email ds@cwu.edu](mailto:ds@cwu.edu) for more information.

**Religious Holiday**

University Policy, CWUP 5-90-040(39), provides for reasonable accommodation of student absences for religious holidays in accordance with [RCW 28B.137.010](https://apps.leg.wa.gov/rcw/default.aspx?cite=28B.137.010). Students seeking reasonable accommodations under this policy must provide written notice to their instructors within the first two weeks of class specifying the dates for which religious accommodations are requested. Contact the Vice President of Student Engagement and Success at (509) 963-1515 for further information.
