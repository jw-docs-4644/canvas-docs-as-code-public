# Lecture Reflections

If you are attending a lecture asynchronously, watch the recorded lecture after it is posted on Canvas, then write and submit a reflection of at least 100 words that addresses the following:

1. **Attentiveness and Engagement:** Demonstrate your attentiveness during the session by referencing specific details from the lecture. How did you actively engage with the material, and how has this engagement contributed to your understanding of the topics we covered?
2. **Capturing Remarkable Elements:** Identify one or two specific parts of the lecture or discussion that stood out to you as particularly remarkable, surprising, or useful. Consider key concepts, insights, or examples that left a lasting impression.
3. **Meta-cognition and Personal Insights:** Reflect on your own learning process. Did anything in the lecture or discussion change the way you think about the course topics? Discuss any personal insights, questions, or connections you made during the learning process.

Your response can take the form of notes you take during the lecture or a more polished response that you craft after viewing. Do not worry too much about crafting perfect prose. Instead, focus on capturing key details and connecting them to your own learning.

The due date will be set as the date of the lecture. You'll have a week to complete your reflection.
